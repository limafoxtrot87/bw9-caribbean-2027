Open risks

Things that could move a number or kill an option. None are settled.

Barceló may bar the 19-year-olds

A Barceló-branded policy reportedly requires a 24+ chaperone for every guest aged 16–23. Found on a third-party site, not barcelo.com — UNVERIFIED. If real, it disqualifies both Barceló properties outright. One phone call settles it.

Resort pricing is an estimate

Nightly rates are verified for the real dates, but family-of-4 occupancy pricing is not — it's modelled at base × 1.35, ±15%. That's wide enough to reorder the middle of the list. Airbnb totals, by contrast, are real checkout prices.

Three nights doesn't work

At 3 nights, Airbnb inventory collapses from 18 listings to 2. Minimum-stay rules, not price, are the constraint. Four nights is the floor for the villa option.

Villa rates are floors, not quotes

Agency "from" pricing is quoted by occupancy — a real 23-person spring-break quote lands higher. Airbnb direct pricing does not have this problem, which is part of why it wins.

Punta Cana nonstop is missing

TPA→PUJ nonstops exist today (JetBlue, Frontier, Breeze) but none are loaded in the March 2027 schedule yet. If one appears, Punta Cana re-enters the race. Re-check around Sept–Oct 2026.

Beds, not bedrooms

A "9-bedroom" villa can have 14 beds. At 23 people that means kids share. The 17-bed listings are the honest fits for this group.
