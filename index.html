Four ways to do this — in plain English.

Pick the style of trip first; the destination comes second. Here's what each approach means and roughly what a family pays. (All per family, 7 nights.)

Simplest & cheapest together

Take a cruise

One ship out of Tampa — no flights, everyone together, kids' & teen clubs on board. You drive to the port. Short port stops, not deep island time.

$0/family & up
Easiest once you land

All-inclusive resort

One hotel; food, drinks, and kids' clubs included. You fly there. Zero planning on-site — just show up. Mid-price, and a few budget islands run cheaper.

$0/family (to ~$11k)
Best value if you'll DIY

Split a villa

Rent big houses together and split the cost — nicest place per dollar. But you handle groceries/a chef and rental cars, and you add flights on top.

$0/family lodging + flights
Best of both

Do two trips

Parents take a spring getaway; everyone does a summer villa week. Two vacations for about the price of one big group trip.

$0/family · both trips
What moves the price, in three lines: (1) Airfare is per person — so bigger families pay more (use the "My family" pull-down above to see your number). (2) How fancy the lodging is — value AI vs. luxury villa can be 3× apart. (3) Kids-free deals — only some resorts, usually under age 12–17, and usually not over spring break. · Closer & cheaper to reach: Bahamas, Puerto Rico, Cancún, Jamaica. Farther & pricier: Turks, Anguilla, Cayman, the French islands.

THE LEVERAGE

Booking twenty-three people at once.

At seven rooms you sit just under most group thresholds (resorts want 10, cruise lines want 8). The fixes are concrete — and one move unlocks nearly all of them.

Put a group travel agent in front of it all

They fold you into existing group inventory below the official minimums and pass the comp rooms / onboard credit down to you — and set up a portal where each family pays their own room. The commission's paid by the resort, not you.

Unlocks
everything ↓

Resort: aim Hyatt (Secrets / Dreams) off-peak

Low/mid-season gives 1 free room per 6 — your 7 rooms already earns a comp room. Spring break blacks it out (drops to 1-per-10), so this favors the summer trip.

~$2,000–3,500
free room

Cruise: book NCL 15+ months out, or add an 8th cabin on Royal

Norwegian clears your 7-cabin count alone and gives 15% off brochure fare at 451+ days. An 8th cabin on Royal triggers a free berth + $50/cabin credit.

~$150–1,500
per group

Flights: use the group desk for flexibility, not discount

Lock 23 seats now with a small deposit, names & balance later, cheap name swaps if a family drops. Southwest's two free bags is a real per-family save.

Skip the 6–15.5% platform fee on a booking this size and negotiate a free night, chef days, or airport transport as a big-booking concession.
